# Cloud Reservoirs

## Current Structure

Each current atmosphere contains four triangular prism reservoirs called clouds.

Known features:

- Gravity-driven tipping mechanism.
- Fulcrum pivot with nylon-glass bearing.
- Reservoirs collect condensate from the chilled rear glass.
- When water mass shifts the center of gravity, a reservoir tips and releases rain into the biome below.
- Adjacent clouds may destabilize and tip sequentially.

## Rain Cadence

- Observed cadence: roughly every 2-3 weeks.
- Cadence varies with climate settings, ambient conditions, condensation rate, and reservoir fill behavior.
