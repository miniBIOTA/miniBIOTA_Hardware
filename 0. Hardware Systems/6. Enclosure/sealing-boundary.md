# Sealing Boundary

## Current State

- Minor passive air leakage exists.
- No routine feeding occurs.
- No nutrient supplementation occurs.
- No filtration or mechanical correction is present.
- Organisms may still be added or removed for experimental refinement.
- Water is not routinely added.

## Target State

- Fully airtight.
- No gas exchange with room air.
- No water addition or removal.
- No feeding.
- No organism introduction.
- Only energy input via light through glass.

## Boundary Definition

Mechanically, final closure means no gas, liquid, or solid matter crosses the system boundary.

Biologically, all trophic energy should be derived from internal cycling and photosynthesis.

Chemically, all elemental cycles should remain contained within system boundaries.

## Boundary Inclusion

Included inside the biosphere boundary:

- Six biome tanks.
- Four atmosphere tanks.
- Internal air volumes.
- Internal water volumes.
- Soil and substrate.
- Organisms.
- Condensation surfaces.
- Rain reservoirs.
- Sealed ports and couplers.

Excluded from the biosphere boundary:

- External lighting fixtures.
- Electrical control systems.
- Chiller hardware.
- External coolant loop hardware.
- Room air.
