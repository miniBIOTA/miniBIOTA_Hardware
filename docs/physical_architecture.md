# Physical Architecture

The structural layout, support systems, and modular connection architecture of the miniBIOTA biosphere.

---

## Core Definition
- miniBIOTA is a modular, closed ecological biosphere system composed of multiple interconnected biomes housed in 29-gallon glass tanks.
- It simulates real-world ecosystem processes including atmospheric exchange, water cycling, nutrient cycling, trophic interactions, and long-term ecological succession without external filtration or feeding once sealed.

## Closure Definition

### Current State
- Minor passive air leakage exists.
- No feeding occurs. No nutrient supplementation occurs.
- No filtration or mechanical correction is present.
- Organisms are occasionally added or removed for experimental refinement.
- Water is not routinely added.

### Target State
- Fully airtight. No gas exchange with room.
- No water addition or removal. No feeding. No organism introduction.
- Only energy input via light through glass.

### Closure Defined
- **Mechanically:** No gas, liquid, or solid matter entering or exiting the system.
- **Biologically:** All trophic energy derived from internal cycling and photosynthesis.
- **Chemically:** All elemental cycles contained within system boundaries.

## System Boundaries

### Included
- All six biome tanks. All four atmosphere tanks.
- All internal air volumes. All internal water volumes.
- All soil and substrate. All organisms.
- All condensation surfaces. All rain reservoirs. All sealed ports and couplers.

### Excluded
- External lighting fixtures. Electrical control systems.
- Chiller hardware. External coolant loop hardware. Room air.

Energy crosses the boundary as light. Thermal gradients are externally influenced but internally expressed. Mass is not intended to cross the boundary in final configuration.

## Structural Support System
- Each biome tank is supported by a custom-built plywood cabinet with enclosed front doors.
- Height varies by biome — creates intentional vertical staggering.
- Elevation hierarchy: Lake < Lakeshore < Grassland = Mangrove > Beach > Seagrass Meadow.
- Elevation staging supports surface water continuity, convection-driven airflow, realistic terrain gradient, and functional hydrological flow logic.

## Atmosphere Mounting System
- Each atmosphere tank is a vertically oriented 29-gallon glass tank mounted above specific terrestrial biomes.
- Supported by a custom wooden bracket resting on the upper perimeter edge of the biome below.
- Atmospheres are atmospheric processing chambers, not biomes.

## Modular Architecture
- Each biome has: 2 ports left, 2 ports right, 1 bottom port, 3 top ports (all 3-inch).
- Ports are permanently drilled bulkheads.
- Connections use 3-inch Fernco rubber PVC couplers and rigid PVC segments.
- The system is modular and expandable. Biomes can be rearranged (requires draining and resealing).

## Biome Configuration (Left to Right)
1. Lake (Freshwater Aquatic)
2. Lakeshore (Transitional)
3. Grassland (Terrestrial Freshwater)
4. Mangrove Forest (Terrestrial Marine)
5. Beach (Marine Shoreline)
6. Seagrass Meadow (Marine Aquatic)

Atmospheres exist above: Lakeshore, Grassland, Mangrove, Beach.
Lake and Seagrass are atmospherically routed to adjacent atmosphere processors.
